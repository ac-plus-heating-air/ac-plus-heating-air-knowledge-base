## What is a central air conditioning system?

A central air conditioning system is a ducted cooling system. It conditions air from a single central unit and distributes it throughout a building via ductwork and vents. The system draws warm indoor air across a refrigerant-filled evaporator coil, removes heat and humidity, then pushes the cooled air back through supply ducts to every room. Central AC systems serve homes ranging from 600 to 3,000 square feet, making them a common cooling solution for single-family residences in the Inland Empire. ## What is a ductless mini split system?

A ductless mini split system is a type of split-system HVAC equipment that delivers conditioned air directly into a room without any ductwork. It pairs an outdoor compressor unit with one or more indoor air handlers, connected by a refrigerant line set routed through a small wall opening. Because there is no duct network to lose energy through, a mini split delivers conditioned air exactly where you need it. ## What is a heat pump?

A heat pump is a refrigerant-cycle device that transfers heat between indoors and outdoors, providing both heating and cooling from a single system. It moves existing heat rather than generating it through combustion. That distinction separates it from a gas furnace entirely. The difference makes it one of the more efficient HVAC options available for climates like the Inland Empire, where winters are mild and summers are long.

## What is a packaged HVAC unit?

A packaged HVAC unit is a self-contained heating and cooling system that houses every major component. Compressor, heat exchanger, and air handler blower. Inside a single outdoor cabinet. Unlike a split system, which divides components between an indoor air handler and an outdoor condenser, a packaged unit consolidates everything in one enclosure. That design makes it a practical fit for buildings without interior mechanical space, including commercial rooftops and slab-foundation homes. As a type of central air conditioning system, a packaged unit connects directly to the building's existing ductwork to deliver conditioned air throughout every room.

## What is a gas furnace?

A gas furnace is a combustion appliance that burns natural gas or propane to heat air, then distributes that heated air through ductwork to every room in a building. Gas furnaces are a type of forced-air heating system, which means they rely on a blower to move conditioned air rather than radiating heat from a surface. In Victorville, CA, where winter nights drop well below freezing in the high desert, a reliable forced-air furnace is the primary defense against cold. ## What is an electric furnace?

An electric furnace, called an electric air handler or resistance furnace, is a forced-air heating unit that warms air by passing it across electrically energized heating elements. It is a type of forced-air furnace, sharing the same duct-and-blower delivery method as gas models but drawing entirely from the electrical supply rather than combustion. Homes in Victorville, CA without a natural gas line rely on this equipment as a primary heat source. ## What is a condenser unit?

A condenser unit, called an outdoor unit, is a type of HVAC equipment that dissipates heat from refrigerant to the outside air. It sits outside the home and forms one half of a split-system air conditioner. The other half lives indoors as an air handler or evaporator unit, which absorbs heat from inside the home before sending refrigerant out to the condenser to release it. ## What is an air handler?

An air handler, called an air handling unit, is a type of HVAC equipment that circulates and conditions air inside a building by moving it across coils connected to heating or cooling equipment. The unit sits indoors and works in tandem with an outdoor condensing unit to deliver temperature-controlled air through the duct system. Most residential air handlers handle between 400 and 2,000 CFM of airflow, depending on the size of the home and the system design.

## What is a blower motor?

A blower motor, called a furnace blower motor, is an HVAC electrical motor component. It spins the fan wheel inside an air handler or furnace, pushing conditioned air through the duct system and into every room of a building. Without it, heated or cooled air stays trapped at the unit. It never reaches the living space. AC Plus HVAC services blower motors across Victorville and the surrounding High Desert communities.

## What is compressor failure?

Compressor failure is the mechanical or electrical breakdown of the refrigerant compressor unit inside an AC system, resulting in a complete loss of cooling capacity. It is a type of HVAC Equipment Failure. The compressor sits at the highest point of the severity scale because it pressurizes refrigerant and drives the entire cooling cycle. Without a functioning compressor, refrigerant stops circulating and the system produces only warm air. AC Plus handles compressor failure diagnosis and replacement for residential and light-commercial systems throughout Victorville, CA.

## What is capacitor failure?

Capacitor failure is the inability of a run or start capacitor to store and release electrical charge within its rated specifications. It is a type of HVAC Electrical Failure, placing it in the same category as contactor faults, wiring shorts, and motor winding failures. When a capacitor can no longer hold its charge, the motor it serves. Compressor, condenser fan, or blower. Loses the electrical boost it needs to start or run. The result is an AC system that struggles to turn on, runs poorly, or shuts down entirely.

## What is short cycling?

Short cycling is a type of HVAC system malfunction in which a heating or cooling unit repeatedly starts and stops before completing a full temperature equalization cycle. The system turns on, runs briefly, then shuts off and restarts within minutes. This pattern is called short-cycling or, less formally, rapid cycling. It is one of the more damaging operating states an HVAC system can enter.

## What is a frozen evaporator coil?

A frozen evaporator coil is an evaporator coil encased in ice, preventing normal heat exchange between the refrigerant and the warm air passing through your AC system. It is a type of Evaporator Coil Condition. A measurable, diagnosable state of the coil rather than a failure of a single component. Ice accumulation on the coil blocks airflow, drops refrigerant pressure below safe operating levels, and forces the rest of the system to compensate until something breaks. AC Plus Heating and Air diagnoses frozen AC coil conditions across Victorville and the High Desert region.

## What is a dirty condenser coil?

A dirty condenser coil is a condenser coil obstructed by accumulated dirt, debris, or biological matter that reduces the unit's ability to reject heat. It is a type of HVAC Component Fouling Condition, the broader category that covers any degraded state in which a heat-transfer surface is blocked by foreign material. The condenser coil sits inside the outdoor unit and releases the heat absorbed from inside your home into the outside air. A fouled condenser coil cannot complete that exchange efficiently, so the entire system works harder to reach the same temperature.

## What is R-410A?

R-410A is sold under the trade name Puron. It is a synthetic hydrofluorocarbon (HFC) refrigerant blend used in residential and commercial air conditioning systems. After R-22 was phased out for its ozone-depleting properties, R-410A replaced it as the dominant cooling refrigerant in the United States. R-410A carries no ozone depletion potential, which made it the standard choice for new equipment through the early 2020s.

## What is R-22 refrigerant?

R-22 refrigerant is a chlorodifluoromethane compound once used as the standard cooling agent in residential and commercial air conditioning systems across the United States. Known widely as Freon 22, it belongs to the hydrochlorofluorocarbon (HCFC) refrigerant class. The EPA banned new production and import of R-22 as of January 1, 2020, under Section 608 regulations. Reclaimed or stockpiled supply is now the only legal source. Victorville homeowners with older AC systems still running on HCFC-22 face a narrowing window before service becomes impractical.

## What is a ductwork leak?

A ductwork leak. Called a duct air leak. Is a gap or failed joint in a duct system. It allows conditioned air to escape into unintended spaces such as attics, crawlspaces, or wall cavities. This places it in the category of HVAC System Defect, alongside failed blower motors and refrigerant loss. Mechanical failures that differ fundamentally from a duct breach. Those are equipment-level problems. A duct breach is a structural failure within the distribution network itself, not a malfunction of the equipment that generates conditioned air.

## What is an air filter?

An air filter, called a furnace filter, is a replaceable media device that captures airborne particulates from the air circulated through a heating and cooling system. Air filters are a type of Air Quality Control Component, sitting at the intersection of indoor air quality and mechanical system performance. The filter media traps dust, pollen, pet dander, and other particles before they reach the blower, coil, and ductwork. Without one, unfiltered return air carries debris directly into the system, accelerating wear and degrading the air your household breathes.

## What is SEER rating?

SEER Rating is a type of Energy Efficiency Rating that measures how efficiently an air conditioning system cools a space over an entire season. The ratio divides total seasonal cooling output, measured in BTUs, by the total electricity consumed, measured in watt-hours, across that same period. Higher numbers mean more cooling per unit of electricity. In the Inland Empire's climate, where cooling loads run high for months at a time, that ratio has a direct effect on how hard an AC system works and how long it lasts.

## What is HSPF rating?

HSPF Rating, formally called the Heating Seasonal Performance Factor, is a type of Heat Pump Efficiency Rating that measures how efficiently a heat pump delivers warmth over an entire heating season. The ratio divides total BTU output by total watt-hours of electricity consumed across that season, so a higher number means less electricity used per unit of heat delivered. HSPF2 is the revised standard the U.S. Department of Energy adopted in 2023, replacing the original test protocol with stricter real-world conditions. AC Plus Heating and Air helps Victorville homeowners understand where their equipment stands against these benchmarks before selecting or replacing a heat pump system.

## What is ENERGY STAR rating?

ENERGY STAR Rating (called ENERGY STAR Certification or EPA Energy Rating) is a type of Energy Efficiency Certification administered by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. The program assigns a certification mark to HVAC equipment and other products that meet or exceed minimum energy efficiency standards set above the federal baseline. Equipment carrying this mark has been verified through third-party performance testing, not self-reported manufacturer claims.

## What is load calculation?

Load Calculation, called a Manual J Calculation, is an engineering process that quantifies a building's heating and cooling demand in BTUs per hour. It is a foundational step in HVAC System Design. This process determines what size and capacity of equipment a home or building actually needs. Without it, every downstream decision about equipment selection rests on guesswork.

## What is NATE certification?

NATE Certification, formally known as North American Technician Excellence Certification, is a standardized industry credential that verifies an HVAC technician has passed rigorous knowledge exams covering installation and service work. NATE-certified status is one of the most widely recognized HVAC technician credentials in the United States. Unlike a manufacturer training certificate, it is issued by an independent third-party organization and tests real-world competency across multiple specialty areas.

## What is EPA section 608 certification?

EPA Section 608 Certification is a federal credential that authorizes HVAC technicians to purchase and handle regulated refrigerants on stationary equipment. It is a type of HVAC technician certification, sitting within the broader category of professional credentials required by federal law before any refrigerant work begins. The Clean Air Act, specifically Section 608, establishes this requirement across all 50 states. No exemptions exist for small jobs or residential systems.

## What is an HVAC permit?

An HVAC permit is an official government authorization required to legally install or replace heating, ventilation, and air conditioning equipment. It is a type of Building Permit, issued by a local building authority before any covered HVAC work begins. Without one, the installation is classified as unpermitted work, which creates legal and financial exposure for the property owner.
