### What is compressor failure

Compressor failure is the mechanical or electrical breakdown of the refrigerant compressor unit inside an AC system, resulting in a complete loss of cooling capacity. It is a type of HVAC Equipment Failure. The compressor sits at the highest point of the severity scale because it pressurizes refrigerant and drives the entire cooling cycle. Without a functioning compressor, refrigerant stops circulating and the system produces only warm air. ### AC compressor failure symptoms and measurable attributes

AC compressor failure follows a predictable pattern. It is defined by three measurable attributes that, taken together, point clearly toward a failed unit. Operating pressure drops to 0 PSI on the high-side gauge. This confirms the compressor has stopped pressurizing refrigerant entirely. Electrical draw climbs more than 10% above the nameplate amperage rating. A reading captured with a clamp meter before the compressor trips its breaker or burns a winding. Refrigerant flow rate falls to between 0 and 20% of rated capacity. Even a system holding a full refrigerant charge delivers almost no cooling under those conditions. These three readings together support compressor failure diagnosis by a trained technician. A system showing all three is not a candidate for refrigerant recharge or filter replacement; the compressor itself requires evaluation.

Secondary symptoms a homeowner notices include a loud grinding or clanking noise at startup, the outdoor unit humming without the fan spinning, and warm air from every supply register regardless of thermostat setting. [Short cycling](https://ac-plus-heating-air.mintlify.site/short-cycling), where the system turns on and off every few minutes, often precedes full compressor seizure by days or weeks and is worth addressing before the compressor locks up completely.

### Component parts involved in compressor seizure

Compressor seizure typically traces to one of three internal or adjacent components. The seized compressor piston is the most severe failure mode; the piston locks inside the cylinder bore, drawing maximum current until the motor burns or the breaker trips. Burned motor windings result from sustained electrical stress, often caused by low refrigerant levels that reduce the cooling effect on the motor itself. A failed start capacitor is the most common upstream cause and the most repairable: the capacitor delivers the surge of current the compressor motor needs at startup, and without it the motor strains, overheats, and eventually seizes. [Capacitor failure](https://ac-plus-heating-air.mintlify.site/capacitor-failure) is worth ruling out first, because replacing a capacitor costs a fraction of replacing the compressor.

### How compressor failure relates to the broader HVAC system

Compressor failure causes a complete cooling system shutdown, which distinguishes it from partial failures like a dirty evaporator coil or a weak blower. When the compressor stops, refrigerant pressure equalizes across the high and low sides of the system. The [blower motor](https://ac-plus-heating-air.mintlify.site/blower-motor) continues running and moving air, but that air passes over an evaporator coil that is no longer absorbing heat, so every room in the building warms toward outdoor ambient temperature. A [frozen evaporator coil](https://ac-plus-heating-air.mintlify.site/frozen-evaporator-coil) can develop in the period immediately before full compressor failure, when refrigerant flow is reduced but not yet zero, causing the coil surface temperature to drop below freezing while airflow remains restricted.

Compressor failure affects refrigerant containment. A seized piston can crack the compressor housing, releasing refrigerant into the refrigerant circuit or, in severe cases, into the surrounding equipment cabinet. ### Compressor failure diagnosis steps

Compressor failure diagnosis follows a defined sequence to separate a failed compressor from a failed capacitor, a tripped breaker, or a low-refrigerant condition. The four steps are below.

- **Electrical check:** Electrical check confirms voltage at the compressor terminals and measures amperage draw against the nameplate rating. A draw exceeding nameplate by more than 10% with correct voltage points to a mechanical or winding fault inside the compressor.

- **Capacitor test:** Capacitor test uses a capacitance meter to verify the start and run capacitors are within rated microfarad tolerance. A failed capacitor mimics compressor failure symptoms and must be replaced and retested before condemning the compressor.

- **Pressure gauge reading:** Pressure gauge reading on both the high and low sides confirms whether the compressor is building differential pressure. A 0 PSI high-side reading with the compressor energized confirms it is not pumping refrigerant.

- **Winding resistance test:** Winding resistance test with an ohmmeter checks the common, start, and run terminals for open or shorted windings. A shorted winding to ground confirms internal motor failure and means the compressor requires replacement.

### Cost of compressor failure repair and the case for replacement

Repair cost depends on scope. The decision turns on whether the compressor is replaced alone or the outdoor unit is replaced entirely. A compressor replacement on a system still under the manufacturer's coverage includes the part, while labor and refrigerant recovery remain out-of-pocket expenses. On a system outside that coverage period, the combined part and labor cost of a compressor swap often approaches the cost of a new outdoor condensing unit, which comes with fresh manufacturer coverage on all components. Technicians weigh the system age, refrigerant type, and efficiency rating before recommending one path over the other.

Compressor operation depends on three conditions: adequate refrigerant charge, clean condenser coils that allow heat rejection, and a functioning start capacitor. Restoration after a seizure may be possible when the failure is caught early, before the motor windings burn or the piston scores the cylinder wall. Full breakdown changes the calculation. A system that has reached full AC compressor breakdown with burned windings and 0 PSI output is not a restoration candidate.
