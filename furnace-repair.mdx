### What furnace repair is and what it is not

Furnace repair is the targeted correction of a specific fault inside an existing heating system. A technician identifies the failed component, replaces or restores it, and verifies that the system returns to normal output. This is distinct from [furnace installation](https://ac-plus-heating-air.mintlify.site/heating-installation), which involves selecting, sizing, and fitting an entirely new unit. Furnace replacement is the right path when a heat exchanger is cracked beyond repair or when the unit is too old to justify the cost of parts. Furnace service, by contrast, keeps a repairable system running rather than swapping it out.

### Common signs that furnace repair is needed

Furnace repair becomes necessary when the system shows recognizable failure patterns. Short cycling. Where the furnace starts and stops before the home reaches the set temperature. Points to a faulty flame sensor or a tripped limit switch. A furnace that runs continuously without reaching temperature often has a cracked heat exchanger or a failing blower motor. Yellow or flickering burner flames, unusual banging or rattling on startup, and a sudden spike in gas consumption are each warning signs that heating system repair should not be deferred. Delay has a cost. Ignoring these symptoms extends the fault to adjacent components and turns a one-hour fix into a multi-part job.

- **Short cycling:** Short cycling signals a dirty flame sensor, a blocked flue, or an overheating limit switch.

- **No heat output:** No heat output is the most severe symptom and often traces to a failed ignitor or a tripped safety control.

- **Yellow burner flame:** Yellow burner flame indicates incomplete combustion and warrants immediate furnace service.

- **Unusual noise:** Unusual noise on startup, such as banging or squealing, points to a loose blower wheel or a failing motor bearing.

- **Elevated gas consumption:** Elevated gas consumption without a change in usage habits suggests the system is working harder than it should to compensate for a fault.
